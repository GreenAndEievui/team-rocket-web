Planning Phase

Abstract

This project is designed to make it easier for people to report lost pets. Instead of having to deal with a phone number, the user just scans a QR code and fills out a couple of text fields. The experience is designed to be the most streamlined for the reporting user. This is to hopefully allow the most reports possible as they require little effort.

The other user end is the app used by people who lost their pet. They fill in the information for their pet and are given a QR code. When someone makes a report that they found the pet, the app sends a notification to the user.

Body

Feature 1: Report when the user loses a pet

The user who lost their pet makes a report on the app. The user scans the QR code to make the report.

Pros:
  • Accessible
  • Easy to report the lost pet
Cons:
  • QR codes could be difficult to scan from a distance.

Feature 2: Forum for found pets

If a user finds a pet, they can post it to a feed that has posts from local users of lost pets. Someone who loses their pet can check this feed as well as the report made with their QR code.

Feature 3: Pet profiles

People can make profiles for their pets. If a similar pet is reported on the forum they can get a notification.

Pros:
  • More convenient for users. They can find their pet without needing to put out the posters.

Conclusion

At this point the plan is to build an app that lets users report lost pets, post about found pets on a public forum, and create profiles for their pets. If local support is needed, we may be able to reach out to PetSmart or other local animal shelters. We will use the waterfall method to create this application, which means we will go through the planning, analysis, design, development, and support phases. Our team will keep files on GitHub and use Discord for communication.
